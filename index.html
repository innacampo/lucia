Redefining Women's Health: From Dismissal to Diagnosis

The Problem: A Crisis of Trust

For decades, women have faced symptom dismissal, diagnostic delays, and chronic misdiagnoses—especially during life-changing health events like menopause. A patient's narrative, rich with context and emotion, is often reduced to a checklist, leading to a breakdown in communication and care. This isn't just inefficient; it's a systemic bias that harms millions.

Our Solution: The Bias-Aware Agent

We're building an AI-powered agent that listens, understands, and validates the patient’s full narrative. It uses advanced Natural Language Processing and a curated knowledge base of clinical data and patient stories to identify subtle symptom clusters and generate a comprehensive, bias-aware report. This report is a tool for the patient, giving them the confidence and data to advocate for themselves.

  • Narrative Analysis: Goes beyond keywords to understand context and emotion.
  • Bias Mitigation: Explicitly flags and contextualizes common forms of symptom dismissal.
  • Evidence Generation: Synthesizes patient-provided data with clinical research.
  • Patient Empowerment: Provides a structured report to facilitate better clinical conversations.

A Look at the Potential

Patient Narrative

For the past 6 months, I've been feeling incredibly exhausted, even after sleeping for 9+ hours. I've also had persistent migraines and sudden, intense mood swings. My doctor told me it's probably just stress from work and suggested I try yoga. I feel like she didn't really listen to me. I'm worried it might be something more, like perimenopause, but I don't feel validated.

Agent-Generated Report

Symptom Mapping

  • Translates "incredibly exhausted" to "chronic fatigue"
  • Translates "persistent migraines" to "hormonal headache"
  • Translates "sudden, intense mood swings" to "emotional dysregulation"

Bias Awareness

The doctor's dismissal of symptoms as "just stress from work" is a common form of diagnostic bias, which often delays appropriate care for perimenopause and other hormonal conditions.

Structured Advocacy

  • "Given my narrative, could these symptoms be related to hormonal changes or perimenopause instead of just stress?"
  • "Could we discuss lab tests, such as FSH and estradiol levels, to rule out or confirm hormonal fluctuations?"
  • "What other conditions could cause this cluster of symptoms, and can we develop a plan to investigate them?"
